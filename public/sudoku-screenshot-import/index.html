Import a Sudoku Puzzle From a Screenshot

Upload a screenshot of one Sudoku grid. Sudoku Pilot reads the large digits and puts them in an editable review screen. Check the grid, fix any OCR errors, and start solving.

By Admin · Updated July 18, 2026

Use a close, straight screenshot

Choose a PNG, JPEG, WebP, GIF, or BMP. Crop the image close to one complete 9 by 9 grid and keep the outside border visible.

Straight screenshots work better than angled photos. Glare, shadows, and decorative fonts can lower recognition accuracy.

Fix the OCR result before starting

Tap any cell in the review grid to add, replace, or remove a digit. Check all 81 cells, including the empty ones.

The app uses the reviewed grid as its starting point. An OCR mistake left here becomes part of the puzzle.

What the importer can read today

The importer is tuned for large, centered digits. It does not import pencil notes. Add those from the solving board after the puzzle opens.

Handwriting is hit or miss. Treat every handwritten result as a draft and check it carefully.

The screenshot stays in your browser

Recognition runs locally. The first scan may download OCR files from Sudoku Pilot, but the selected image is not uploaded to an OCR service.

After review, the imported puzzle gets the same notes, hints, and technique tools as a generated puzzle.

Tips for a cleaner scan

  • Crop close to the outside edge of the Sudoku grid.
  • Use a straight-on image with even lighting.
  • Keep the full border visible.
  • Review all 81 cells before starting.
  • Enter pencil notes after the import is complete.
